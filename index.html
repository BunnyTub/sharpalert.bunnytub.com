SharpAlert works day and night, to alert and protect. It can be used in the home, on public displays, TV stations, and much more.

Simplifying a bit, SharpAlert works similarly to how Wireless Emergency Alerts would work or your phone. In the background, SharpAlert constantly polls for new alerts. When an alert is received, the program checks it against your preferences. If the alert is allowed to relay, a visual and auditory message plays. It tells you the event type, and information about the alert. You can play TTS (Text-To-Speech) or embedded audio (included within some alerts) by clicking the play button. TTS or embedded audio will be played automatically if you have SharpAlert in full screen mode.

SharpAlert (by default) receives alert information from IPAWS CAP.
